## TL;DR

Open Tactics is a **Fire Emblem-style tactical RPG** with excellent technical foundation but needs visual polish and content to become marketable. With **12 months of focused development** and a budget of **$2-4K**, it can launch as a successful premium mobile game.

**Current State:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4/5) - Core mechanics work, needs polish  
**Technical Health:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5) - Clean code, good tests, CI/CD  
**Market Readiness:** ⭐⭐ (2/5) - Functional but not polished enough

## What We Have

### ✅ Strengths
- **Production-ready game engine** with comprehensive tactical RPG mechanics
- **26% test coverage** (19 test files) with 100% passing builds
- **Excellent CI/CD** (GitHub Actions with automated testing, linting, security)
- **Clean architecture** enabling easy feature additions
- **5 playable character classes** with leveling and progression
- **Full combat system** including weapon triangle, criticals, terrain effects
- **Save/load system** with encrypted persistence
- **Support relationships** and trading between allies
- **Healing system** with EXP rewards

### ⚠️ Gaps
- **No sprite graphics** - Text-based units only
- **No audio** - Completely silent
- **Limited content** - Basic chapters without story
- **Basic UI** - Functional but not polished
- **No marketing presence** - No community or awareness

## Why This Can Succeed

### Market Opportunity
- Tactical RPG fans want premium games without predatory F2P
- Fire Emblem on mobile (Heroes) makes $100M+/year but is gacha-based
- Premium tactical RPGs scarce on mobile (Tactics Ogre, Banner Saga successful)
- Growing indie game movement on mobile

### Competitive Advantages
1. **Player-first philosophy** - No dark patterns
2. **Open source** - Community can contribute
3. **Classic mechanics** - Fire Emblem gameplay proven
4. **Mobile-optimized** - Touch controls from day one
5. **Quality code** - Professional architecture and testing

### De-risking Factors
- Core gameplay already validated (works well)
- Low development cost (modest budget needed)
- Existing CI/CD reduces deployment risk
- Soft launch option to validate before full launch
- Passionate niche audience exists

## Risk Factors

### High Risk
- **Market saturation** - Many tactical RPGs exist  
  *Mitigation:* Differentiate via open source and player-friendly model
  
- **Content production** - Creating 20 chapters is significant work  
  *Mitigation:* Start with 12-15 chapters MVP, add more post-launch

### Medium Risk
- **Solo development** - Bus factor of 1  
  *Mitigation:* Document everything, engage community early
  
- **Platform changes** - Android API deprecations  
  *Mitigation:* Stay current with androidx libraries

### Low Risk
- **Technical debt** - Minimal, clean codebase  
- **Core mechanics** - Already validated and tested
